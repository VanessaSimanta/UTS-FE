Do You Know? Nancommunicable Diseases Kill 41 Million People Each Year Because Of Poor Lifestyle

Noncommunicable diseases (NCDs), also known as chronic diseases, are the result of a combination of genetic, physiological, environmental and behavioural factors. The main types of NCD are cardiovascular diseases (such as heart attacks and stroke), cancers, chronic respiratory diseases (such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and asthma) and diabetes.

Based on WHO, NCD are mostly caused by modifiable behavioural risk factors such as unhealty diet, tobacco use, physical inactivity, harmful use of alcohol and then metabolic risk factors such as raised blood pressure, overweight/obesity, hyperglycemia, hyperlipidemia and lastly environmental risk factors such as air pollution.
